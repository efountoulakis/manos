About me

Hello! I am Emmanouil, but actually everybody calls me “Manos”. I was born in 1991 in Heraklion, a city of the beautiful island of Crete, Greece. I received the M.Eng. from the school of electrical and computer engineering at the Technical University of Crete (TUC) in July 2016. In August 2016, I joined the MT group at LiU as Marie Curie Ph.D. fellow where currently I am pursuing my Ph.D. degree in wireless networks under the supervision of Prof. Nikolaos Pappas. I spent 1,5 years in Nokia, Bell-Labs, Stuttgart, as an early stage researcher.

Research

My research interests include:

  • Age of Information

  • Low-latency Communications

  • Energy-efficient Resource Allocation

  • Applying Queueing Theory

  • Stochastic Network Optimization

  • Markov Decision Processes
